## Overall result

11 of 11 page citations across both cases are correct. Every obligation
in the report points to the page in the source PDF where the cited
Article actually begins. A reader can verify each obligation by opening
`data/ai_act.pdf` to the cited page.

This is the property the obligations report needed to be trustworthy as
decision-support. It now has it.

## How the per-system "Why this applies to your system" notes were assessed

Each obligation in the high-risk case includes a 2-3 sentence note
generated by Groq Llama 3.3 70B explaining why the Article applies
specifically to a CV-screening tool. Notes were spot-checked for:
- factual accuracy (Article 9 actually does require risk management for the full lifecycle: confirmed)
- system-specific reasoning (notes reference CVs, bias, ranking algorithms, human recruiters: confirmed)
- absence of false-confidence language (no claims of legal certainty: confirmed)

The notes are LLM-generated and clearly distinguished in the report from
the hardcoded obligation specifications. The disclaimer
("This is decision-support, not legal advice. Verify with qualified counsel.")
appears at the end of every report.
